## How Big Is TikTok Shop Indonesia in 2025?

**TikTok Shop Indonesia generated $13.1 billion in gross merchandise value in 2025, making it the platform's second-largest market globally after the United States ($15.1 billion).** The entire Southeast Asia region reached $45.6 billion in TikTok Shop GMV, effectively doubling from the prior year. Indonesia alone accounts for roughly one-third of that total.

To put this in competitive context: according to Momentum Works, Shopee Indonesia led the overall e-commerce market at approximately $25.8 billion in GMV, followed by Tokopedia — which ByteDance acquired in December 2023 to re-enter the Indonesian market — at approximately $14.0 billion. Lazada Indonesia trailed at $4.5 billion. TikTok Shop's 111% growth rate, however, far outpaced all three incumbents.

Data from the impact.com and Cube Asia *E-commerce Influencer Marketing in Southeast Asia 2025* report shows that **76% of Indonesian consumers have purchased products through creator-recommended content**, the highest rate among all six Southeast Asian markets surveyed. Meanwhile, according to PaymentsCMI research, **60% of Indonesian consumers report having completed a purchase during a livestream session**.

Transaction patterns reveal additional opportunities. **Peak purchasing hours on TikTok Shop Indonesia fall between 6:00 and 9:00 PM local time (WIB)**, with that three-hour window accounting for roughly **30% of daily transactions**. Average monthly e-commerce spend among Indonesian consumers sits at approximately **$35 per user**, and the best-performing product price range on TikTok Shop lands between **$3 and $16**.

## What Content Strategy Works Best for TikTok Shop Indonesia?

**Creator-led content with price anchoring in the first three seconds of a video consistently doubles click-through rates compared to generic discount claims**, based on MOCA Technology's campaign data across Indonesian TikTok Shop campaigns.

The psychology is straightforward. Indonesian consumers are value-conscious but skeptical of generic discount messaging. A side-by-side price comparison — showing a product at $12 on TikTok Shop versus $18 on Shopee or a traditional marketplace — gives the viewer an immediate, concrete reason to act. The comparison does the persuading; the creator provides the trust.

This shifts the content strategy from "talk about how cheap we are" to "show the math." ## Why Does Cultural Localization Matter for TikTok Shop Indonesia?

**Indonesia has the world's largest Muslim population — approximately 87% of its 280 million citizens identify as Muslim, according to Indonesia's national census — making Halal certification and cultural fluency non-negotiable requirements for any brand selling through TikTok Shop.**

MOCA Technology has been deeply cultivating the Indonesian market for years, and our on-the-ground teams have identified patterns that remote operations consistently miss:

**Livestream greetings matter.** Top-performing hosts open with "Assalamu'alaikum" (the Islamic greeting of peace) before any product discussion. This is a trust signal that measurably affects viewer retention in the opening seconds.

**Certification drives conversion.** Indonesian consumers actively look for two badges: **BPOM** (Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan, Indonesia's national food and drug authority) and **MUI Halal** certification (issued by Majelis Ulama Indonesia, the Indonesian Ulema Council). Creators who display both certifications on-screen during product demonstrations see higher add-to-cart rates than those who mention product quality in general terms.

**Timing requires cultural awareness.** Scheduling livestreams or pushing notifications during prayer times damages brand perception. Indonesia's five daily prayer periods are non-negotiable windows to avoid. Brands that build their content calendars around these schedules earn stronger long-term audience loyalty.

## What Licenses and Certifications Do Brands Need to Sell on TikTok Shop Indonesia?

**To sell on TikTok Shop Indonesia, foreign brands must operate through a local-to-local model:** establishing a PT (Perseroan Terbatas, the standard Indonesian limited liability company structure), registering products with BPOM, obtaining SNI (Standar Nasional Indonesia) product certification, and securing MUI Halal certification where applicable.

The practical requirements include:

| Requirement | Description | Timeline |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Local entity (PT PMA)** | Foreign-owned limited liability company registered in Indonesia | 4–8 weeks |
| **Import licenses (API/API-U)** | Required for bringing products into the country | Included in PT setup |
| **BPOM registration** | Mandatory for cosmetics, food, health supplements, pharmaceuticals | 2–12 months |
| **MUI Halal certification** | Required for food, beverage, and personal care products | 3–6 months |
| **SNI compliance** | Mandatory for electronics and select consumer goods | Varies by category |
| **Local warehouse** | In-country fulfillment infrastructure required by TikTok Shop | Ongoing |

In MOCA Technology's experience working with international brands entering Indonesia, companies that treat regulatory setup as a first-phase priority reach profitability significantly faster. Every compliance requirement that slows down a competitor becomes a moat for brands that have already done the work.

The October 2023 ban is a reminder that regulatory risk in Indonesia is real. Brands should not treat policy stability as a default assumption.
